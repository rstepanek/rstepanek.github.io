Neural Network powered car - In plain javascript!

Neural Networks are a powerful tool in AI and Datascience. Functioning in a matter not entirely dissimiliar to our own brains, Neural Networks are a flexible and powerful choice for classification and data based decisioning.

In this demo, I have implemented a simple neural network in plain javascript. While the neural network is unoptimized and small, it can still yield powerful results. Here you can help train a self-driving car as it learns to drive on a simulated road using "real" simulated sensors!

On the left side you will see the car in action and on the right side you will see the neural network of your current best car. On the right side, the bottom layer corresponds to the sensor inputs of the vehicle, while the top layer shows the direction in which the vehicle is traveling (up, left, right, down).

Play around with the settings and have fun. See how quickly you can train the car to navigate the highway! Click "Next Sim" to begin.

Tips and Tricks

  • This demo can be computationally intensive if the settings are adjusted too high. If you encounter performance issues, click the checkbox to activate "Low-Res Mode" on your next simulation run.
  • Before the cars have really began to learn you will notice a lot of "bumper riding" behavior. Just set a high mutation rate and click "Next Sim" to get better model behavior in the next run.
  • The camera follows the "most fit" AI car. This is not always the car in the lead, for example passing cars in traffic is worth more fitness points than bumper riding.
  • Your best overall model is saved between rounds and used as the baseline for the next round. If you restart the sim, the best model is reset as well.
  • Number of parallel simulations is capped at 500.
  • The mutation rate must be a value between 0 and 1. The higher this value, the more the behavior of your AI cars will diverge from your current best model. If you have a model that is almost, but not quite making it through traffic, then you want only a slight mutation rate: try changing the mutation to a value between 0.1 and 0.16.
  • Number of sensors is capped at 9. You should restart the simulation after changing this value as inputs to the neural network will have changed.

K-Means Clustering Demo

K-means clusting is a very useful technique for grouping data. While there are other means of clustering data, the K-means algorithm has acheived enduring popularity due its simplicity and linear scaling, enabling it to work with large datasets.

Below is a small demo where you can step through the K-means algorithm or let it run to see all the steps through completion. This is an intuitive way to play the algorithm and see it adjust centroids (here represented by the 'x's) at each step as new nodes (circles) are added and removed from the clusters.

At each step, the central point of each cluster (centroid) is recalculated and nodes that are now closer to the new centroid are moved into the group. This continues until until the centroid positions no longer change. Play around with the settings below!
